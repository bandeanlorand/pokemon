Charizard leads the charge in the
Pokémon TGC: XY-Flashfire expansion!

Over 100 Trading Cards including five new Pokémon-EX
& three new Mega Evolution Pokémon!

Share:

Ignite the action with Fire-type Pokémon as the awesome Mega Charizard-EX makes its scorching debut in this breathtaking expansion. Your explosive adventures will continue as your foes flee the field with certain doom flashing before their eyes!

While Charizard-EX deservedly gets most of the spotlight in the XY—Flashfire expansion, it's not the only Pokémon worth noticing. In fact, there are three more Pokémon-EX in the expansion that are all strong in their own ways: Magnezone-EX (101/106) can deal major damage to your opponent's bench, Toxicroak-EX (102/106) dishs out a particularly powerful Poison, and Kangaskhan-EX (103/106) can help you draw cards when you need them. Check them out!

7/106

Shiftry

Even if the Grass-type Pokémon Shiftry is on your Bench, it can have a huge impact on your game. Its Leaf Draw Ability lets you quickly fill your hand: if you discard a Grass Energy, you can draw 3 cards. And when Shiftry attacks with Deranged Dance, you have the potential to deliver up to 200 damage in one blow! That's enough to Knock Out even a Pokémon-EX in a single hit!

12/106

Charizard-EX

Add some firepower to your deck with Charizard-EX. It brings two powerful attacks: Wing Attack for 60 damage and Combustion Blast for a scorching 150. And if that isn't enough damage to take care of your opponent's Pokémon, remember that Charizard-EX can evolve into Mega Charizard-EX, a nearly unstoppable force!

13/106

Mega Charizard-EX

Mega Charizard-EX does one thing, and it does it very well—massive damage. This Fire-type Mega Evolution Pokémon has only one attack, but it's a doozy! Crimson Dive does a devastating 300 damage, enough to topple the most sturdy Pokémon. Crimson Dive does come with a hefty Energy cost, and it does 50 damage to Mega Charizard-EX, but with 220 HP to start with, this Pokémon can take damage nearly as well as it can dish it out.

20/106

Pyroar

Because Pokémon-EX are heavily featured in the tournament scene right now, a lot of decks are filled with Basic Pokémon. You can dramatically slow their advances with Pyroar, a Stage 1 Pokémon whose Intimidating Mane Ability protects it from damage done by your opponent's Basic Pokémon. That Xerneas-EX that does 140 damage (XY, 97/146) won't leave a scratch on Pyroar. Pyroar has some bite, too—its Scorching Fang attack does a base 60 damage, with the option to kick it up to 90 if you discard an attached Fire Energy.

23/106

Milotic

Milotic makes for some interesting strategic choices. Its Energy Grace Ability lets you attach three basic Energy cards from your discard pile to one of your Pokémon—but you have to Knock Out Milotic to use it. If Milotic is low on HP, instead of letting it take that last hit, use this Ability to put your next Pokémon in a better position to go on the offensive. That might just be enough to turn the tide of battle in your favor.

40/106

Dusknoir

The Stage 2 Dusknoir is definitely a card for players who like to take risks. Dusknoir's Shadow Void Ability sucks up damage counters from your other Pokémon. You can then follow up with its Pain Pellets attack to dish out damage counters to an opposing Pokémon equal to the number of damage counters on Dusknoir. On the one hand, you can heal your other Pokémon and then do up to 120 damage to your opponent's Pokémon! On the other hand, Shadow Void is a one-way street—Dusknoir is stuck with all that damage.

49/106

Barbaracle

Attacks like Barbaracle's Rock Rush fill Pokémon TCG players with dreams of wild and fun Energy-heavy decks, and for good reason. Rock Rush does 30 damage times the number of Fighting Energy you discard from your hand, meaning there's no Pokémon you couldn't Knock Out if you had enough Energy. More conventional players may instead choose to attack with Boulder Crush, which does 80 damage. Pretty impressive for a Stage 1 Pokémon!

60/106

Forretress

The disadvantage of many tougher Pokémon is high Retreat Cost, and Forretress looks to exploit that disadvantage even further. Its Iron Crash attack does 20 damage plus an additional 20 for each Colorless Energy in the target's Retreat Cost. Against Venusaur-EX (XY, 1/146), for example, Iron Crash does 100 damage for just 2 Colorless Energy! Forretress has one more trick—its Thorn Tempest Ability lets you put a damage counter on each of your opponent's Pokémon when you play it to evolve one of your Pokémon. Every little bit helps!

64/106

Floette

69/106

Mega Charizard-EX

Like the Fire-type version, the Dragon-type Mega Charizard-EX has a furious attack that does an incredible 300 damage in Wild Blaze. Wild Blaze has a pretty hefty Energy requirement, and you must discard 5 cards off the top of your deck. But when you start Knocking Out your opponent's Pokémon each turn, those drawbacks will probably be of little concern as you march toward victory.

71/106

Dragalge

The power of Poison continues with the Dragon-type Pokémon Dragalge. Its Ability and attack work well together: The Poison Breath attack does 60 damage and can Poison the opponent's Active Pokémon. Then you can trap your foe with Poison Barrier, which prevents Poisoned Pokémon from retreating. Note that Dragalge doesn't have to be Active for Poison Barrier to work—keep it on the Bench and use Hypnotoxic Laser (Plasma Storm, 123/135) to prevent Pokémon from retreating at will.

79/106

Mega Kangaskhan-EX

With Mega Kangaskhan-EX, huge damage isn't guaranteed as with many other Mega Evolution Pokémon. However, there's a lot of potential and far fewer downsides. Mega Kangaskhan-EX's Wham Bam Punch does 100 damage to start with, and then you flip a coin until you get tails and add 30 damage for each heads. That's 3 Colorless Energy and no penalties for an attack that starts at 100 damage and could go as high as the sky!

80/106

Snorlax

For a Basic Pokémon, Snorlax is both powerful and complex. Its Sleepy Press attack does 120 damage and heals 20 damage from Snorlax, both very useful. But the attack also makes Snorlax sleep, and its Stir and Snooze Ability makes you flip two coins instead of one to wake it up! Try out Snorlax's high-risk, high-reward approach and see if it fits your style of play.

83/106

Miltank

Miltank might not be a Pokémon you can build a whole deck around, but it's the perfect secondary Pokémon for some decks. Be sure to include this Pokémon if you're playing a deck with several Stage 2 Pokémon. When one of them is on your Bench, Miltank's Powerful Friends attack increases from 10 damage to 80 damage, and it costs only 1 Colorless Energy. Miltank even has a decent 100 HP, making it bulky enough to withstand a direct hit or two.

85/106

Lopunny

Pokémon TCG players love Pokémon that give them options, and Lopunny does just that. During your turn, you can return Lopunny (and all the cards attached to it) back to your hand via its Big Jump Ability. Whether Lopunny has taken a lot of damage or you just want to reclaim some of the Energy attached to it, you can pull it back in a pinch. This is especially useful if you lose the coin flip for its Sitdown Bounce attack: next turn, when Lopunny can't attack, you can Big Jump it back to your hand and attack with a different Active Pokémon!

88/106

Blacksmith

Give a boost to all the excellent Fire-type Pokémon in the XY—Flashfire expansion with the Blacksmith Supporter card. It lets you pull 2 Fire Energy cards from your discard pile and attach them to one of your Fire-type Pokémon. A lot of the attacks on Fire-type Pokémon not only have high Energy requirements but also make you discard Energy when you use them. Use Blacksmith to recharge!

89/106

Fiery Torch

If you're playing a Fire-type deck, you can keep the flow of cards coming with the Fiery Torch Item card. Fiery Torch lets you discard a Fire Energy to draw 2 cards. This card works well as a counterpart to the Blacksmith Supporter card, which gets Fire Energy cards out of the discard pile and onto your Pokémon.

100/106

Charizard-EX

The highlight of Charizard-EX is its fantastic scorching 120-damage Fire Blast attack. But this potent attack costs 4 Energy to use. Fortunately, Charizard-EX helps itself out with the Stoke attack, which lets you attach 3 Basic Energy cards from your deck to Charizard-EX after a successful coin flip. Under the right circumstances, you could be hitting for 120 damage on turn two!

101/106

Magnezone-EX

There aren't many Lightning-type Pokémon in the XY—Flashfire expansion, but they're all pretty great. Take Magnezone-EX and its Dual Bullet attack, for example. With this attack, you can hit any 2 opposing Pokémon for 50 damage, leaving your opponent with nowhere to hide.

102/106

Toxicroak-EX

Attacks and effects that Poison your opponent continue to be popular, with Toxicroak-EX being the latest and greatest example. For only two Colorless Energy, you can use Triple Poison, which has three times the potency of the regular Poison condition. For added effectiveness, put Dragalge (71/106) on your bench to make sure your opponent's Active Pokémon can't retreat.

103/106

Kangaskhan-EX

No matter what deck you're running, Kangaskhan-EX can be a great addition. It has everything you need: lots of HP, some tactical help, and a fairly potent attack. Start off with the Triple Draw attack to draw 3 cards. Then, pivot into damage mode with the Kindred Kick attack, which does a base 70 damage plus 30 more on the result of a coin flip. The best thing about these two attacks? They require only Colorless Energy, making Kangaskhan-EX versatile enough to slip into pretty much any deck.

104/106

Lysandre

Lysandre's icy stare lets your opponent know trouble is coming. This Supporter card lets you switch out your opponent's Active Pokémon for a Benched Pokémon of your choice. Pick the matchup that suits you best, and give it everything you've got!

105/106

Pokémon Center Lady

Get your Pokémon back in top shape with the Pokémon Center Lady. This Supporter card heals 60 damage and removes all Special Conditions, perfect for helping Dusknoir (40/106) recover after using its Shadow Void Ability, or cleaning up after Toxicroak-EX’s (102/106) Triple Poison attack.

106/106

Pokémon Fan Club

Got a bunch of Pokémon-EX (or other strong Basic Pokémon) that you're eager to get into battle? Play the Pokémon Fan Club Supporter card to get 2 Basic Pokémon from your deck to your hand. Obviously Pokémon Fan Club is a great way to set up early in a match, but it can also come in handy when you're behind late in a game and looking for a way to make a comeback.

There are a lot of powerful cards in the XY—Flashfire expansion, but make no mistake—this is Charizard's time to shine. With two Charizard-EX and two Mega Charizard-EX to build a deck around, you have plenty of ways to develop winning strategies with the awesome Flame Pokémon.

The two Charizard-EX have their own strengths. The first (11/106) allows you to get Fire Energy from your deck easily, and the second (12/106) has more ways to do greater damage.

The two Mega Charizard-EX open up a range of deck-building opportunities based on their different types: one is a Fire-type Pokémon (13/106), and the other is a Dragon-type Pokémon (69/106). No matter which you choose to use, you can't go wrong—each has more than 200 HP and an attack that does a staggering 300 damage!

Get ready to scorch your competition with the combination of power and flexibility of the new Charizard cards in the XY-Flashfire expansion!

M Charizard-EX (69/106) + Sacred Ash (96/106)

Don't deck yourself out when attacking with the Dragon-type M Charizard-EX! Every time you attack with Wild Blaze, you have to discard five cards off the top of your deck.

One quick way to keep your deck full is the Sacred Ash Item card, which lets you move five Pokémon from your discard pile to your deck.

M Charizard-EX (13/106)+ Protection Cube (95/106)

The biggest threat to the Fire-type M Charizard-EX is perhaps itself. The Pokémon takes 50 damage every time it uses its awesome Crimson Dive attack.

Negate that damage with the Protection Cube, a Pokémon Tool card that prevents any damage done by a Pokémon's own attacks! Now there's no drawback to using Crimson Dive every single turn.
